How to Make a Chocolate Cake!

What kind of cake will you be making?

The cake you will be making will be a Hershey chocolate cake. It will, of course, be a chocolate cake covered in chocolate buttercream frosting. The cake itself will be nice and moist and is perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients, or What You Will Need:

The cake and the frosting require different types of ingedients, so they will be listed separately. Here are the ingredients for the cake:

  • 2 cups of white sugar
  • 1 and 3/4 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 of a cup of unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 and 1/2 teaspoons of baking soda
  • 1 and 1/2 teaspoons of baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup of milk
  • 1/2 of a cup of vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons of vanilla extract
  • 1 cup of boiling water

Ingredients needed for the frosting:

  • 3/4 of a cup of unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 5 and 1/3 cups of confectioners' sugar
  • 1 and 1/2 cups of unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2/3 of a cup of milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

The Directions, or How to Make The Cake and Frosting:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350 degrees fahrenheit or 175 degrees celsius. While your oven is heating up, grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.
  2. Stir the dry ingredients: the sugar, flour, cocoa, baking soda, baking powder, and salt, in a bowl
  3. Add the eggs, milk, oil, and vanilla to the dry mixture. Mix it all for roughly 3 minutes or until homogenous, then add in your boiling water and stir it too. Remember to be careful!
  4. Pour your batter into the two pans you greased and floured earlier
  5. Bake both pans in the oven for 30 to 35 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean when inserted into the center of the cakes. Cool them for 10 minutes before removing them from the pans to let the cakes cool completely.
  6. Now it's time to make the frosting! Stir the unsalted butter with a mixer until it is light and fluffy. Add in your confectioners' sugar and cocoa with your milk and vanilla and beat it til smooth.
  7. Now that your frosting is complete, cut each cake in half horizontally. You should now have 4 equally-sized circular cakes.
  8. Now stack the 4 cakes on top of each other and add frosting in between each layer to act as an adhesive of sorts for the layers.
  9. Frost the outside of the cake with your remaining frosting. Congratulations! Your cake is now complete and ready to serve!
